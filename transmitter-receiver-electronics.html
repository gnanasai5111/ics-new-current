Transmitter and Receiver Electronics

The Transmitter and Receiver Electronics is used to detect the objects within the range 10KM and calculate distance from the objects to the source by using LASER light. The design implemented measures the range information based on the time taken for a light pulse to reach a target and echo back. This design comprised of a light emitting circuit that outputs short and powerful pulses of laser beam toward a measurement target object; a light receiving circuit that photo-electrically converts the laser light reflected from the target object and amplifies the converted optical signal; a time to digital converter (TDC) to calculate the TOF of the laser light. Hardware designed architecture is based on Spartan 6 FPGA and micro controller for required application.

Transmitter and Receiver Electronics Features and Applications

Features

  • Spartan 6 series FPGA for developing the Logic of distance finder.
  • ST Micro controller is used for monitoring & controlling the DC supplies.
  • A separate Photo Detector Card is used for receiving the laser signal and converting in to electrical.
  • A PIN Photo Diode card is used to for converting the electrical signal to optical signal by using the Driver card module.
  • 512Kbyte flash memory for threshold.
  • Range Gating Circuit.
  • 30MHz reference clock for building the logic.
  • RS422 Interface for User Interface to GUI.
  • RS232 Interface between FPGA and Controller for command and data transfer.
  • Time Programmable Gain control for APD Circuit.
  • Input supply of the board is 24V DC and separate regulators are used for generating FPGA voltages.

Applications
